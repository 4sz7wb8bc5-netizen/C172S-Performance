Utility category requires both a lighter max weight (2200 lb) and a tighter, more-forward CG range than normal category — it's the envelope pilots use to stay approved for spins and other utility-category maneuvers. A point inside the amber line only is labeled normal category; inside the cyan line as well is labeled utility category. The C172S has no published zero-fuel-weight limit — this point is shown for reference, to see how much the fuel load itself shifts the CG.

If a fetch fails, your browser or network may be blocking the request to ECCC/MSC — conditions can still be entered by hand on the Takeoff/Landing/Climb pages.

On sourcing: NAV CANADA's own Aviation Weather Web Site was decommissioned in Feb 2024, and its replacement (CFPS on plan.navcanada.ca) doesn't publish a public, cross-origin-accessible API — it's a login-oriented flight-planning portal, not something this page can query directly from your browser. The Canada Flight Supplement is similarly a paid publication with no public API. METAR/TAF above are instead pulled directly from ECCC/MSC (Environment and Climate Change Canada's Meteorological Service) — the official Government of Canada agency that writes these reports in partnership with NAV CANADA — via their free, public MSC GeoMet data service. Aerodrome elevation comes from OurAirports, a free public-domain database. Magnetic variation isn't fetched — enter it by hand on the Takeoff/Landing pages. Each section above also links straight to NAV CANADA's official CFPS Weather Recall tool so you can cross-check the report yourself.

Surface & Configuration

Edits here are permanent for this browser. Changing a tail's empty weight or moment (e.g. after seats or equipment are removed) updates every calculation that uses it from now on, on this device — including the Weight & Balance page whenever that tail is selected. Use "Reset to POH default" to undo a change.
Advisory tool only. Distances are bilinearly interpolated from POH short-field performance charts and are not a substitute for the aircraft's actual POH, a weight & balance sign-off, or an official weather briefing. Always verify against your POH before flight.
